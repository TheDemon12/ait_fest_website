Robo War

Description

"Fight smart , be patient and pray!"

The time has come for the bots to battle and take away our breaths! Who's going to be on top? Will it be Noisyboy? Or is there a Zeus or an Atom among you? Think your bot has what it takes to beat them all?

Robo Weight :

5kg with 10% tolerance

Basic Rules

  1. There are no restrictions on the dimensions
  2. The maximum allowed volatge is 12V DC
  3. Both wired and wireless bots are allowed
  4. The length of the wire should be atleast 5 m in case of a wired bot
  5. No active weapons are allowed due to safety concerns
  6. Extra 10 points will be awarded to wireless bots.

Fight Rules

  1. Two teams have to fight with each other according to the number of attacks by each team, points will be allocated.
  2. Any team that will make the opponent team inactive for more than 20 second then that team will earn extra bonus points.
  3. Points rules will be declared at the time of the event.

Robo Race
Robo Race

Description

Life is a race where everyone wants to win. All work hard to be a winner in their own sphere.
Here’s a good news for all Technos out there for a stress free and fun loving race : Roborace.
Everyone is amazed by the speedy cars like Veynom , veron etc.
If you wish to design your own fastest vehicle, Roborace is the right platform for you.

Design a manually controlled ROBOT that has capacity to cover maximum distance in the shortest possible time, challenging the hurdles and be one of the best among all. Think your robot can overcome any obstacle-big or small in the least amount of time. If so get it on the track and let the game begin.

Robot Specications

  1. The maximum dimension of the robot can be 30 x 30 cm (l x b).
  2. The robot may be wired or wireless.
  3. The length of the wire (for wired bots) should be 5m or more and wire should remain slack during the complete run.
  4. Maximum weight must not exceed 5 kg.
  5. Only 220V AC  power supply will be provided at the site.
  6. Maximum allowed voltage to power the robot is 24V DC.

Rules and Rounds

  1. This is a racing event so the fastest and most balanced robot will be the winner.
  2. Maximum of 4 members are allowed per team.
  3. No test practices will be allowed on the main arena.
  4. Handling of more than one robot by any member of a team is strictly prohibited.
  5. Every team should have their distinct robot.
  6. The robot should not damage the arena, doing so, the penalty time of 30 sec will be added.
  7. First hand touch is allowed with no penalty. For each forthcoming hand touch, penalty time of 15 sec will be added further to overall time.
  8. If any robot leads to malfunctioning during the run , then a maximum of 10 minutes will be given to the team. If the team fails to repair the robot within the time limit, then it may lead to disqualification.
  9. Once on track no readjustment and modifications in the robot will be allowed.
  10. The track surface and course line may have unevenness. There will be certain obstacles in track which will try to slow down the robot.   
  11. There will be different bonus time for each obstacle.
  12. There will be a penalty of 30 sec for skipping an obstacle.
  13. The decision of judges will be final and binding.
  14. Argument with the judges shall lead to disqualification of the team.

Line Following Robot

LFR
Line Following RObot

Description

Have you wondered ; how ants always travel in a line, following an invisible route in search of food, or back home .
Ever thought about a robot which follows line?
A perfect or near perfect mimic of nature?
After all the purpose of robotics is to recreate in terms of machines what one loves in nature.

Rules

  1. Three members are allowed per team.
  2. The dimensions of LFR(Bot) must not exceed 18 x 18 x 15cm (l x b x h)
  3. You may use whatever motors and chassis you want, but BOT must not be disastrous to people or cause damage to the track surface.
  4. Any number of sensors can be used on the LFR.
  5. The BOT must operate autonomously. Remote controlled LFRs are not allowed.
  6. Codes must not be hard coded and the teams should be able to explain the code to Event Heads.
  7. Any group found cheating or not following the guidelines shall be declared unfit for the event.
  8. Any kind of argument with the event members shall lead to disqualification.
  9. Event Heads decisions are final and binding.
  10. In case the Event Heads points something is not okay then their ruling stands even if the item wasn't originally listed in the rules.

About the Event

  1. Width of black line will be approx. 2 cm.
  2. Marking Crteria
    • A bonus of 500 points will be credited for successful completion.
    • A bonus of 200 points will be credited for completion without any repositioning.
    • 100 point will be deducted if the bot leaves the arena.
